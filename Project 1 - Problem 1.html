Problem 1 introduction

The problem we solved is perhaps best known as the secretary problem. Assume that there is a company looking for a new employee. What is the probability of choosing the best candidate among a set of applicants? If one assumes that the order of the candidates is entirely random and that once a person is rejected, he or she is rejected for ever, it can be shown that there is a strategy that will choose surprisingly often the best candidate -- or at least one of the top three candidates.

Briefly, the strategy is to discard the k first candidates, find the best candidate among the k rejected candidates, x*, and choose the first person after the kth canddate that is better than x*.

The reason for this is that P(Z = 1|Y = i) is the probability that the strategy will pick the best candidate when the position of the candidate is known to be i. However, the strategy will never pick the right candidate as long as the best candidate is among the k candidates rejected. Therefore, the probability is P(Z = 1|Y = i) is 0 for all i less than i less than k + 1.
